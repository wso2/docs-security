### AFFECTED PRODUCTS
* WSO2 API Manager: 4.2.0, 4.1.0, 4.0.0, 3.2.0
* WSO2 Enterprise Integrator: 6.6.0
* WSO2 Micro Integrator: 4.3.0, 4.2.0, 4.1.0, 4.0.0, 1.2.0


### OVERVIEW
Potential MiTM due to missing Websocket hostname verification.


### DESCRIPTION
Due to hostname verification being disabled  a malicious actor could intercept the gateway to backend websocket connections to perform a MiTM attack.


### IMPACT
In order for the attack to succeed  a malicious actor should have access to the network connection between the gateway and the backend application. 

By leveraging the vulnerability  a malicious actor could intercept and potentially alter the communication between two parties  gaining unauthorized access to sensitive information such as login credentials  personal data  or confidential messages. This could lead to unauthorized actions  identity theft  or the compromise of sensitive transactions.


### SOLUTION

#### Community Users (Open Source)
Migrate to the latest unaffected version of the respective WSO2 product(s).


#### Support Subscription Holders

Update your product to the specified update level, or to a higher update level, to mitigate the identified vulnerability.
